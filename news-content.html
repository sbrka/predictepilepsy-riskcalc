News & updates

predictepilepsy.com is a centralized, freely available platform bringing together commonly used prognostic calculators for epilepsy. This page tracks new calculators, features and improvements. Most calculators were developed by others; we provide their implementation here for ease of access — please see our Disclaimer and methodology.

Jul
2026

🩺Presurgical & SUDEP toolset added

The library now covers presurgical planning and SUDEP risk: the Jehi nomogram for seizure freedom after epilepsy surgery, the Cleveland Clinic nomograms for naming, verbal-memory and mood decline after temporal-lobe surgery, the 5-SENSE score for stereo-EEG focality, and a set of SUDEP tools — the SUDEP-3 inventory, a personalised SUDEP relative-risk model, and a SUDEP risk-marker reference. Browse all calculators →

Jul
2026

💊Individualised ASM-withdrawal nomograms

Added the Lamberink individualised prediction model, estimating the risk of seizure recurrence (at 2 and 5 years) and the chance of long-term seizure freedom after withdrawing antiseizure medication in a seizure-free patient. Open the calculator →

2026

🧭Redesigned, guided calculator finder

All prognostic calculators are now organised in a modern, guided finder — grouped by clinical situation (after an acute brain injury, after a first or unprovoked seizure, stopping antiseizure medication, epilepsy surgery, and SUDEP risk) — so you can reach the right tool in a couple of clicks.
